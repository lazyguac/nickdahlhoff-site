I have a long history with marijuana and tend to be all-or-nothing with it. There's a big niche for quitting smoking, but a lesser-known one for people who want to use but have more control. I'd found a subreddit where people share resources that aren't very good — a popular app gets recommended regularly that could probably be built in an afternoon with current tools. Bought the domain a year or two earlier but pursued other projects instead.

## What it does

Voice-first journaling focused on cannabis consumption, mood, spending, and patterns. You talk through what's on your mind and the AI extracts structured data from the free-form entry. No manual logging, no forms. The app visualizes that data over time: consumption trends, mood correlations, streak tracking.

All the features work — journaling, AI processing, voice input, dashboards, and community features. Built with Cursor and Claude Code, deployed on Netlify with a Convex backend. It didn't take long to build — a side project, not full-time work.

## The honest assessment

I don't actually think it's a very good idea. The problem: you don't want a marijuana journaling app, you want a journaling app. It can't compete as a general journaling app. I don't even use it myself — it doesn't fit into my own daily journaling practice.

The tracking and visualization part is the real value. But for long-term success, it probably needs a community component — people moderating together. I don't want to build or manage a community for this.

I tried posting about it on the cannabis moderation subreddit. The link got banned. I didn't care enough to try other channels. It has zero users.

## Why it's here

PPP shows that I can go from idea to a deployed, functional product in days using AI development tools. The app itself isn't a business, but the speed and capability it demonstrates is worth showing.
